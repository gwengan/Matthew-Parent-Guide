Matthew's practice app

Please read this guide before running Matthew's first session. Keep it handy for reference.

This guide explains how Matthew's speech practice app works, what you need to do during each session, and how to record his progress so his speech therapist can track how he is going over time.

2  Practice schedule
  1. 1
    Practise at least 3 days per week. Aim to spread sessions out — for example Monday, Wednesday and Friday — so Matthew has time to rest between sessions. Even short, consistent sessions are more effective than one long one.
  2. 2
    Go through all five categories every session. Each time Matthew practises, he needs to complete all five categories — Food & drink, Everyday items, People, Activities, and Places — at least once. He can do them in any order.
    If Matthew is having a difficult day and cannot manage all five, that is okay — but record (screenshot) every category he does complete, and note that the session was cut short.
  3. 3
    Encourage the retry round. When the app offers to repeat the tricky phrases, try to encourage Matthew to have another go. This is one of the most useful parts of the practice.
3  What to record — screenshots every session

After Matthew finishes each category, the app shows a session report. You must screenshot this screen before moving on to the next category. This is the main way Matthew's speech therapist reviews his progress.

Minimum per week
3 sessions × 5 categories = 15 screenshots per week. Every session, every category — no exceptions. If a screenshot is missing, that category cannot be counted toward his progress goals.

4  Carer involvement — especially the first two weeks

A parent or carer must be present for every session during the first two weeks (at least the first 6 practice sessions). After that, Matthew may work more independently — but you should still be nearby during each session.

  1. 1
    Learn how the app works. The first two weeks are your opportunity to get comfortable with the flow so you can run sessions confidently and help Matthew if he gets stuck. You should understand how to start a category, what each rating means, how to override a rating, and how to take the session screenshot.
  2. 2
    Make sure Matthew is using the app correctly. He should be looking at the phrase card and saying the phrase clearly and aloud into the device microphone. Watch for:
    • Whispering or speaking too quietly
    • Looking away from the screen while speaking
    • Rushing through phrases without reading them
    • The mic picking up background noise or your voice
    A quiet room with the TV off gives the best results from the microphone.
  3. 3
    Override the app's rating when it is wrong. See Section 5 below — this is one of your most important jobs during every session, not just the first two weeks.
5  Overriding the app's rating

The app uses a microphone to judge Matthew's responses automatically — but it is not perfect. It can struggle with Turkish words (like Nene, Teyze, Abi, lahmacun), quiet speech, or background noise. You know Matthew's speech better than any microphone does. Trust your ears.

Situation What to do
Matthew said the phrase clearly and correctly, but the app gave Nearly or Keep going Tap the ✓ Got it button to correct it.
The app gave ✓ Got it but Matthew's production was unclear, incomplete, or accidental Tap Nearly or Keep going to correct it.
You realise you need to change the rating of the previous phrase after moving on Tap the ← Go back button (grey, below the blue Next button) to return to the previous phrase and change its rating.
How to override — step by step
After Matthew speaks, the three rating buttons are always visible on screen. Simply tap the button that matches what you heard — ✓ Got it, Nearly, or Keep going. The app updates the rating immediately. Then tap the blue Next → button to continue to the next phrase.
6  How to run each phrase — step by step

Follow this sequence for every phrase in every category. The goal is one clear, recorded attempt per phrase — with one opportunity to practise first if Matthew needs it.

  1. 1
    The question is presented on screen and read aloud. The app will automatically speak the question (e.g. "What do you want to eat?"). You can tap the blue speaker button on the question card at any time to hear it again.
  2. 2
    Practise the response with Matthew — one time only. Show Matthew the phrase on screen (e.g. "I want chocolate") and say it clearly together so he hears how it should sound. This is his one practice opportunity before recording begins.
    Important: allow only one practice attempt before recording. Matthew may not yet know every phrase — that is completely expected and is exactly what the programme is tracking. It is fine for him to make mistakes. We need to capture those mistakes so that when he improves, the progress is visible in the data.
  3. 3
    Signal that recording is starting. Say to Matthew: "OK, are you ready? I'm going to ask again — this time I will record."
  4. 4
    Tap the speaker button to repeat the question, then tap the microphone to record. Tap the blue speaker icon on the question card so Matthew hears the question again. Then tap the large microphone button and let Matthew say the phrase aloud. The app will judge his response automatically.
  5. 5
    Override the rating if needed, then tap Next. Check the rating the app gave. If it is wrong, tap the correct button — ✓ Got it, Nearly, or Keep going. Then tap the blue Next → button to move to the following phrase.
Known app issue — do not tap the microphone a second time
Once a recording result has appeared, do not tap the microphone button again for the same phrase. Due to a bug in the app, tapping the microphone a second time will cause it to get stuck in recording mode — the button stays red, no result appears, and the rating buttons become unresponsive.

If this happens: tap ← Go back, manually tap the correct rating button for that phrase, then tap Next → to move forward. This bypasses the stuck state.

The safest approach: record each phrase once only. If you are unsure about a result, use the rating buttons to override it rather than re-recording.
7  Tips for a good session
  1. 💡
    Find a quiet spot. Background noise — TV, music, other conversations — confuses the microphone and leads to inaccurate ratings. A quiet room is the single biggest thing you can do to improve accuracy.
  2. 💡
    Use the speaker button freely. There is a blue speaker icon on the question card. Tap it to hear the question read aloud at any point — during practice or just before recording.
  3. 💡
    Keep it positive. Even Keep going phrases are progress — Matthew is attempting them, which is exactly the goal. Celebrate effort as much as accuracy.
  4. 💡
    There is no time limit. Matthew can take as long as he needs to look at the phrase and prepare before speaking. Do not rush him.
Quick reference — session checklist
Before you start
  • Find a quiet room — TV and music off
  • Matthew is comfortable and ready
  • Device volume is up so Matthew can hear questions read aloud
During the session (for each category)
  • Matthew looks at the phrase card and says it aloud
  • Encourage the retry round for phrases not yet achieved
  • Wait for the session report screen to appear
  • One practice attempt with Matthew, then record once (see Section 6)
  • Override the rating if the app got it wrong, then tap Next
  • After all 7 phrases: take a screenshot showing all three sections (numbers, phrase list, and written summary)
  • Label the screenshot with today's date and category name
After the full session
  • You have 5 screenshots — one for each category
  • All screenshots show the numbers, phrase list, and written summary
  • Screenshots are saved and labelled
  • Send or share screenshots as agreed with Matthew's speech therapist
